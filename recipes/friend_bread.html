Amish Friendship Bread

Description

This is a traditional recipe that dates back to 1981.
Patience is a virtue with this recipe!

Starter Recipe

Starter Ingredients

  • 0.25 oz active dry yeast (or 1 packet)
  • 0.25 cup warm water 110F / 45C
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup milk

Starter Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in water. Let stand 10 minutes.
  2. In a 2-quart glass, plastic or ceramic container, combine 1 cup flour and 1 cup sugar. Mix thoroughly with a whisk or fork.
  3. Slowly stir in 1 cup milk and dissolved yeast mixture.
  4. Cover loosely and let stand at room temperature until bubbly. Consider this Day 1 of the 10-day cycle. For the next 10 days, handle starter according to the instructions for Amish Friendship Bread.
  5. The starter should be left at room temperature. Drape loosely with dish towel or plastic wrap. Do not use metal utensils or bowls. If using a sealed Ziploc bag, be sure to let the air out if the bag gets too puffy.
  6. Your yield may exceed 4 cups depending on the temperature of your kitchen and eagerness of your starter. If this happens, reserve one cup for baking and divide the remaining starter into Ziploc baggies of 1 cup each to freeze for future use or share with friends.

Do not refrigerate the starter. It is normal for the starter to rise and ferment. If air gets into the bag, let it out.

Loaf Recipe

Preparation

  • Day 1: Do nothing.
  • Day 2: Mash the bag.
  • Day 3: Mash the bag.
  • Day 4: Mash the bag.
  • Day 5: Mash the bag.
  • Day 6: ADD to the bag:
    • 1 cup flour
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1 cup milk
    • Then mash the bag.
  • Day 7: Mash the bag.
  • Day 8: Mash the bag.
  • Day 9: Mash the bag.
  • Day 10: Follow the directions below:
    1. Pour the entire bag into a nonmetal bowl.
    2. Add 1.5 cup flour, 1.5 cup sugar, 1.5 cup milk.
    3. Reserve 1 cup of starter in the bowl if you plan to bake the same day.
    4. Divide the remaining starter into 1-gallon Ziploc bags with 1 cup per bag. Your starter may yield 4 to 7 cups depending on how active your starter has been.
    5. Label your Ziploc bag with the date, starting over at Day 1.

Baking Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup oil
  • 0.5 cup milk
  • 1.5 cup sugar
  • 0.5 teaspoon pure vanilla
  • 3.5 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1.5 teaspoons baking powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 or 2 small boxes instant pudding (any flavor)
  • 1 cup chopped nuts or raisins (optional)

Baking Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325F (165C).
  2. In a bowl mix 0.5 cup sugar and 1.5 teaspoons cinnamon. Set aside.
  3. Add all remaining ingredients to a bowl with the one cup of starter.
  4. Grease two large loaf pans.
  5. Dust the greased pans with the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  6. Pour the batter evenly into loaf or cake pans and sprinkle the remaining sugar-cinnamon mixture on top.
  7. Bake for one hour or until the bread loosens evenly from the sides.
